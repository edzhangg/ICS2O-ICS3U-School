Someone who changed the world with Computer Science: Tim Berners-Lee

Tim Berners-Lee's Contributions to Computer Science:

Tim Berners-Lee is known to be the inventor of the World Wide Web.When he was working at CERN (as a software engineer), he designed and developed a procedure that allows computers to communicate with each other. This was to provide researchers to share results, techniques, and practices without having to constantly send emails, and can be accessed at anytime, anywhere. This would lead to the first web server being created. Then, he went to MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology) to establish the World Wide Web, which billions of people use all over the world today.

Tim Berners-Lee's Career:

Tim Berners-Lee's Career Timeline:

June - December 1980 Worked at CERN, as a software engineering colsultant
1981 - 1984 Worked at Image Computer Systems Ltd, designed computer systems
1984 - 1994 Worked at CERN, designed computer systems
1994 - present Works at MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology), as a professor for computer science

Definitions:
  • CERN: European particle physics laboratory in Geneva, Switzerland - where Tim Berners-Lee created the World Wide Web
  • Software Engineering Consultant: Hired as a contractor for a period of time for a specific task, which usually is to design and develop programs for companies

Tim Berners-Lee's Educational Background:

Tim Berners-Lee went to the University of Oxford.
  • He graduated in 1976.
  • He graduated with a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree in physics.
  • Here, he learned the basics of computing and computer science, and learned physics, which he applied when working at CERN.

Other interesting facts about Tim Berners-Lee:

  • Tim Berners-Lee is a British citizen, who was born in London, England
  • Tim Berners-Lee recieved many awards in his lifetime, including:
    1. Knighted by H.M Queen Elizabeth in 2004
    2. Election as a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 2009
    3. The first Queen’ Elizabeth Prize for Engineering in 2013
    4. Won A.M. Turing Award in 2016
    5. Received more than 10 honorary doctorates
    6. Awarded UK’s Order of Merit
  • His parents worked in the computing field, on the Ferranti Mark I, which is the first commercial computer.
